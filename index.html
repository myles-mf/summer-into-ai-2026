WEEK 2 · INDEPENDENCE ENGINES 8 builds
★ Perpetual Union
The graphics finale, rendered on your GPU. A million points of light — each its own sovereign agent following only its own pull, no conductor, no central control — together hold one shape: the waving flag of the union. Drag across them and they scatter into chaos, independence pulling every which way; let go, and with no one giving the order, they find their way back into one. Real-time GPU particle simulation (a million agents, additive HDR bloom, a flag that waves in 3-D). Out of many, one — and the one survives being torn apart. A union isn't held together from the top; it's an equilibrium a half-million free parts return to on their own.
★ Sovereign
Independence as real cryptography. What makes a union legitimate with no king to stamp it? Consent — proven by math. All thirteen colonies generate their own key in your browser, no server (genuine ECDSA P-256, Web Crypto) and sign their ratification into one tamper-evident chain. Then forge one: change a single colony's signed words and that seal — and every seal after it — turns red, rejected by every honest node with no central authority to ask. Become a sovereign yourself: generate a key, sign your own declaration, and watch the proof die the instant you alter a character. Independence as self-sovereignty — answer to no one, prove it to anyone.
★ Emergent Revolution
Thirteen colonies, each a reinforcement-learning agent hand-written from scratch (no libraries, no LLM, no cloud), starting from zero knowledge. Every round each one chooses: stay loyal and pay the Crown's tax, or revolt. Revolt alone — crushed; revolt together — trade free and the Crown can't hold. Nobody tells them to rebel. They learn it, and a revolution cascades down the seaboard. Slide Tax, Retaliation, and Trade and the learned outcome flips between a loyalist coast and 1776. Independence as an equilibrium that has to be discovered — freeze the learning and the revolution never comes.
★ Self-Evident
A real GPT, hand-written from scratch in one file — attention, backpropagation, and the optimizer, no libraries, no API. It starts as pure noise and teaches itself the voice of the American founding live, while you watch: the loss falls, the gibberish resolves into 1776 cadence, and you see the attention learn. Independence as self-origination — an intelligence with no pretrained ancestors and no cloud. (Backprop verified by a built-in gradient check.)
★ Murmuration
A live starling flock with no leader — order emerges from the bottom up. Slide the dial from every bird free to one Crown, then strike a node: cut a free bird and the flock routes around it; cut the Crown and the whole sky falls. Independence isn't a symbol here — it's the architecture that survives the strike.
★ Liberty Engine
A self-playing anthem with no conductor. Six voices, each on its own independent loop (4–16 beats) that never quite realign — built from scratch with the Web Audio API. Mute any voice, even all but one, and the rest play on. Out of many independent loops, one song. Shareable by seed.
★ SOLO — an A.I. that learns to fly
A real neural net, hand-written in one file (no libraries), learns to fly — either from watching you pilot, or by teaching itself from scratch (neuroevolution, no teacher at all). Then it flies a course it's never seen. Watch the brain fire live. Independence = the moment it no longer needs you.
★ Dependency Day
A systems puzzle — cut the Crown's supply lines so independence cascades, then secure each free state before it's re-garrisoned. Freedom is the win condition.
